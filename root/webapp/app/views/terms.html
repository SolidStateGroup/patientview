Security and Confidentiality

We are committed to protecting the confidentiality of your medical information. PatientView limits staff’s access and ability to enter or view information based upon their role in your care. Firewalls, passwords, secret words, encryption, audit trails, and external testing are further used to safeguard your information.

PatientView records the time and date any user accesses PatientView. It also monitors which records staff access. We have taken steps to make all information received as secure as possible against unauthorized access and use.

PatientView must be accessed with a Secure Sockets Layer (SSL) compatible browser or terminal. PatientView’s SSL web server uses authentication and high levels of encryption technology.

You can tell when you are securely connected to the web server by looking at the location (URL) field. If the URL begins with https:// (instead of http://), the document comes from a secure server. This means your data is encrypted, and is not easily deciphered by unauthorized parties. You can tell whether you are truly connected to PatientView by viewing the digital certificate. This certificate verifies the connection between the PatientView server's public key and the server's identification.

Usernames, passwords and secret words provide three layers of authentication and passwords/secret words are stored in an unreadable (‘hashed’) form. As a PatientView user, keeping your information confidential depends on keeping your username and password/secret word confidential. It is important to make sure the password/secret word you select is easy for you to remember, but difficult for someone else to guess. Do not use your birth date, address, telephone number, names of spouses, children, family members, pets, or any other easily guessed information. Your password must be a minimum of 7 characters in length, ideally containing at least one number and punctuation mark. It is recommended you change your password/secret word on a regular basis.

Remember to sign out of your account and close your browser window when you have finished your session. This is to help ensure that others cannot access your personal information and correspondence if you share a computer with someone else or are using a computer in a public place where others may have access to it.
